S11 · Edge Audit

Edge Audit is a fixed-scope, 4-week engagement that builds or validates measurement infrastructure for systematic quant operations — independently, without a measurement team.

Most systematic strategies are built with strong opinions about market behavior and weak instrumentation around whether those opinions hold. The gap is not in the thesis — it is in the discipline around testing it. We instrument that gap. We produce a structured audit of what you are measuring, what you are missing, and whether your current measurement stack can surface degradation before it becomes a drawdown.

What We Measure
Fill Quality and Execution Leakage

Slippage attribution, venue-level fill analysis, and the difference between theoretical and realized entry and exit cost.

Signal Stability and Regime Sensitivity

Whether the conditions under which a strategy was developed persist in live trading — and how quickly departure from those conditions becomes detectable.

Capacity and Market Impact

At what size the strategy's own execution begins to move the market it is trading. Relevant earlier than most operators expect.

Measurement Layer Completeness

What is being logged, what is not, and what gaps exist that would prevent retrospective analysis if performance degrades.

Operational Consistency

Systematic comparison of intended execution versus actual execution across venues, sessions, and instrument states.

Who This Is For
For
Systematic operators running live capital with no dedicated measurement function
Quant funds or family offices evaluating a manager's infrastructure quality before committing capital
Institutional desk teams with execution infrastructure but no formal audit process
Protocol and market-structure teams running liquidity operations without structured performance instrumentation

You do not need to be large. You need to be running live capital on a systematic basis with an honest question about your measurement layer.

Not For
Retail traders looking for direction on what to trade
Anyone seeking signals, strategy recommendations, or forward-looking projections
Operations without a live systematic strategy already running
Anyone evaluating whether to enter a market — this is not a market research product
Deliverables
01 · Report
Measurement Audit Report

A structured document mapping current measurement coverage against a binary-scored framework across five infrastructure dimensions. Covered, partially covered, not covered. No qualitative hedging.

02 · Register
Gap Register

An explicit list of instrumentation gaps, ranked by operational risk and reconstruction difficulty. Each gap is documented with description, affected dimension, and risk classification.

03 · Specification
Logging Specification

Written specification of what should be captured, at what granularity and retention, to support adequate retrospective analysis of the in-scope strategy. Infrastructure specification, not an implementation guide.

04 · Summary
Decision-Ready Summary

A one-page principal-facing document summarizing audit findings. Format suitable for internal governance review or allocator reporting. No editorial opinion on strategy viability — only on whether the measurement layer supports that decision.

We do not deliver: strategy opinions, execution recommendations, revised signals, or implementation services.
Process — Four Weeks
Week 01
Scoping and Data Access

We align on strategy parameters, execution venues, and available log history. Client provides structured data access per the agreed input list. We confirm scope and flag any data gaps immediately.

Week 02
Instrumentation Audit

We map existing measurement against the five audit dimensions. Binary scoring at each checkpoint — covered, partially covered, not covered. No qualitative hedging.

Week 03
Gap Analysis and Reconstruction

Where gaps exist, we assess whether historical reconstruction is possible and what it would require. We document what is knowable and what has been permanently lost to inadequate logging.

Week 04
Report Assembly and Review

Draft report delivered for principal review. One revision cycle. Final report and Decision-Ready Summary issued at end of week.
